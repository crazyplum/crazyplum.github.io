Projects

  • Multilingual Collaboration Support

    Short Intro: To support problems people facing in multilingual collaboration, we designed a tool that team members could annotate on automated speech recognition transcription, and we conducted an experiement to evaluate whether the design could improve their communication.

    Research Methods: lab study (within-subject design), interview, conversation analysis

    Result: We've found some significance in the quantitative data (survey and task performance), and now I'm working on analyzing the participants' transcription to check how the annotation tool improve their communcation quality.

  • Social Annotation and Second Language Reading

    Short Intro: To support second language readers, social annoatoin may be a possible aid. Therefore, we did an exploratory study to examine how social annotation affects second language reading.

    Research Methods: lab study, retrospective think alound, interview

    Result: We found that social annotation could serve as cognitive aids for second language processing as expected. However, there're social concerns regarding ownership of the article, and the perceived value of consuming notes that are originally intended for personal use only.
